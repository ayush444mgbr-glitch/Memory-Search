Title: Iran vs Israel War

Introduction
The Iran–Israel conflict is one of the most significant geopolitical tensions in the Middle East. The rivalry between the two countries has existed for decades and is driven by political, ideological, and military differences. Over time, this tension has evolved from indirect confrontation to direct military clashes.

Background of the Conflict
The conflict began after the Iranian Revolution in 1979, when Iran adopted a strong anti-Israel stance. Since then, both countries have been involved in a long-standing rivalry. Israel views Iran as a major threat because of its nuclear program and support for militant groups such as Hezbollah and Hamas. Iran, on the other hand, opposes Israel’s policies in the region and supports groups that resist Israeli influence.

Proxy Conflicts
For many years, Iran and Israel fought indirectly through proxy groups and regional allies. Iran supported militant organizations in Lebanon, Syria, and Gaza, while Israel conducted airstrikes and covert operations against Iranian military targets and weapons supplies in these regions.

Escalation into Direct Conflict
In recent years, tensions escalated significantly. Israeli airstrikes targeted Iranian nuclear and military facilities, while Iran responded with missile and drone attacks. Recent Developments
In 2026, the conflict intensified with large-scale airstrikes and military operations involving Israel, Iran, and sometimes the United States. Several strategic locations in Iran were targeted, and Iran launched retaliatory attacks on military bases and regional targets. Global Impact
The Iran–Israel war has serious global consequences. It threatens stability in the Middle East, disrupts oil supplies, and increases tensions between major world powers. Many countries and international organizations are calling for diplomatic solutions to avoid further escalation.

Conclusion
The Iran–Israel conflict remains a complex and dangerous geopolitical issue. Peaceful negotiations and international cooperation are necessary to prevent further violence and ensure stability in the region.
